Problem

It answered the topic, not the question.

A keyword flattened distinct research needs into the same templated report. Users could neither shape the answer nor verify it.

Process

Three inputs changed the product direction.

Customer behavior, product issues, and technical constraints pointed to the same gap: retrieval worked, but Deeli could not interpret intent or return an answer researchers could confidently use.

Solution

Four decisions turned search into a research partner.

I designed Deeli as a research instrument, not a chatbot: calm at entry, dense where evidence matters. Each decision closes one gap: express, aim, observe, verify.

Take the question, not the keyword.

Researchers could ask naturally, including fragments, long questions, and bilingual queries, without translating their intent into search syntax.

Decision: preserve the original query and show the system’s interpretation separately. Silent rewrites would break trust.

Observed: 70% of live queries used forms the old box could not support.

Learnings

Twenty-eight percent of queries were re-asks.

Twenty-six of 92 queries repeated an existing intent. Some reflected healthy exploration, but near-identical questions also produced inconsistent reports. Users were sometimes fishing for an answer they had already seen.

The shipped clarification helped researchers aim one report, but it did not preserve their corrected interpretation across repeated asks.

Next: turn one-time clarification into a persistent interpretation checkpoint. Let researchers confirm or correct entities and scope, then reuse that corrected intent on every re-ask.
